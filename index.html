Dissertation for MSc in Computer Science

Genetic Algorithms in the search for optimal Kuhn Poker strategies


This dissertation examines the search for intelligent computer strategies in the game of Kuhn Poker, a simplified Poker variant that is played between two players with a three-card deck. A Genetic Algorithm (GA) was fully implemented to do this, as well as a Kuhn Poker game, and they were both combined to evolve strategies for the players. The effects of different types of population initialization, selection and crossover were studied.

The results showed that Genetic Algorithms can be quite efficient at generating winning strategies against a particular player by modeling its behaviour. For instance, in a game against a standard player, the best strategies evolved made $0.23 on average if playing first and $0.32 if playing second (the second player generally has an advantage), in a game with $1 bets.

Some of the computer player strategies produced displayed bluffing and sandbagging behaviours as a means to take advantage of the opponent’s habits - highlighting the importance of deceptive game-play in Poker.

Child Rescue Alert

Windows Universal App for CRA initiative


Child Rescue Alert (CRA) is an alert network that aims to notify as many people as possible in the event that a child goes missing. The network is the joint responsibility of CEOP (a command of the National Crime Agency) and the charity Missing People. Groupcall, our client for this project, is the technology provider in charge of managing the automatic distribution system for these missing child alerts.

The objective of the project was to produce a Windows Universal application for Child Rescue Alert. The application alerts users when children go missing in the UK, in order to maximize the reach of child rescue operations. This app constitutes the last of a family of products developed for this purpose (a website, iOS and Android applications and desktop application already exist).

In creating this app, we aimed to make as much use as possible of UI elements and code packages provided by the Windows SDK. This allowed us to focus our efforts on the portions of the code which were specific to our app. The most important of these specialised features were the retrieval of missing child alerts from a JSON API provided by the client, and the receipt of incoming push notifications when new missing child alerts are activated.

The use of design patterns and object-oriented design principles, in particular the Model-View-Viewmodel architecture, allowed us to begin by creating a Windows Phone version of the app and easily add a tablet version later on in development, and will also enable future extension or reuse of the code.

OxHack Finalist, Major League Hacking

Chromecast Alarm Clock application for Android


In a team of four people at OxHack, a hackathon organised at Oxford University by Major League Hacking, we worked together to develop an Android app. This app consisted on an alarm clock that switches on Chromecast on your TV and plays personalised content to wake the user up. We were selected as finalists, and presented our application to over 150 people at the event's closing ceremony.

Unfortunately, after doing some research we learnt that Chromecast can only turn on TVs through HDMI-CEC; an option that isn't available in most TVs and that is disabled by default. For this reason we decided not to move the project forward. Nonetheless, it was a great experience from which we learnt a lot, not only about Android apps with Chromecast, but also about teamwork.

Dissertation for BSc in Mathematics

Applications of the Cantor Set in Topology and Functional Analysis


The main goal of this bachelor thesis was to present and explore the Alexandroff-Hausdorff Theorem, together with one of its most important applications in functional analysis: the Banach-Mazur Theorem. This required making use of a wide range of mathematical tools: weak topologies, dual spaces, the Tikhonov, Banach-Alaoglu and Hahn-Banach theorems, etc.
